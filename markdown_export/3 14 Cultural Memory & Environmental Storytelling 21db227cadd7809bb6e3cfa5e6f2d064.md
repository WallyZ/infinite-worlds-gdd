# 3.14 Cultural Memory & Environmental Storytelling

Cultural memory and oral tradition shape how societies understand their world.

- **Oral Histories & Stories**: Passed through bards, storytellers, and elders — may contain half-truths.
- **Localized Beliefs**: Legends are not global; each culture may interpret the same event differently.
- **Discovery Through Play**: Myths may lead to real locations, ancient powers, or misinformation.
- **Tabletop Analogy**: Inspired by myth tables, regional lore, or legend-tracking in systems like *Symbaroum* or *Numenera*.
