# 12.4.1 Community Governance

### Overview

While *Infinite Worlds* encourages radical creativity, the **Lore Council** exists to maintain consistency across fantasy tone, gameplay systems, and simulation balance — ensuring that community content feels like it belongs in the game’s world, even across procedurally generated saves.

This isn’t about strict canon or persistent timelines, but about curating **quality, lore-consistent contributions** that won’t shatter immersion or break game systems.

---

### 🎯 **Purpose of the Lore Council**

- Ensure that community-created **spells, psionics, skills, monsters, and items** remain:
    - **Aesthetically and thematically appropriate**
    - **Systemically balanced** for both solo and multiplayer
    - **Compatible** with the underlying simulation and progression systems
- Prevent game-breaking or lore-clashing mods from being surfaced as "trusted" or "recommended"
- Create a **lightweight but trustworthy signal** for players browsing mods — "this fits and won’t wreck your game"

---

### 🧙 **The Lore Council**

- A volunteer-based group of **experienced modders, designers, and lore-minded players**
- Collaborates with devs to **review and tag** mods submitted for optional curation
- Operates out-of-game, with tools integrated into the mod submission and browsing flow

### Council Responsibilities:

- Review mod submissions that **opt in** for inclusion in curated lists
- Approve tags such as:
    - ✅ *Lore-Compatible*
    - ⚖️ *Balance-Safe*
    - ⚙️ *System-Integrated* (e.g., AI, ecology, crafting)
    - 🧪 *Experimental* (flagged for creative but potentially unstable systems)
- Flag mods with critical issues (e.g., crashes, infinite loops, broken UIs)

---

### 🧱 **Curation Criteria**

Mods don’t need to match a specific canon — instead, they must:

- **Feel like they belong** in a grounded fantasy simulation (aesthetic tone)
- **Use similar systemic logic** (e.g., new psionic abilities must integrate with effort-based casting, stamina drain, or mental states)
- Avoid:
    - Modern or sci-fi assets unless lore-justified (e.g., ancient tech ruins)
    - Overpowered creations that trivialize survival, progression, or exploration
    - Infinite-resource loops or NPC behaviors that break emergent systems

---

### 📘 **Submission & Tagging Process**

1. **Creator Submits Mod for Review (Optional)**
    - Through an integrated uploader or external site like Nexus
    - Provides details: intended use, balance profile, tone inspiration, system hooks
2. **Council Review**
    - Reviews content manually and via automated test pass
    - Adds curated tags and publishes summary feedback (e.g., “Balanced but heavy on combat focus”)
3. **Visible in In-Game & External Browsers**
    - Players browsing mods can sort or filter by curated tags
    - Optional curated packs feature only council-approved content for immersive-first players

---

### 🛡️ **Why It’s Optional, but Valuable**

- *Infinite Worlds* remains fundamentally open — you can run anything locally.
- But for players who want a **trusted baseline of immersive, balanced experiences**, council-tagged content acts like a seal of trust.
- Especially useful for:
    - Multiplayer hosts
    - Content streamers
    - Lore-conscious roleplayers
    - Skill-learning challenge players

---

### 🔁 **Recommended Mod Categories for Review**

- **New Spells & Psionics**
    
    Must adhere to the player’s effort system, gesture/voice casting, and affect fatigue or mental focus
    
- **New Skills or Knowledge Trees**
    
    Must fit into progression through experimentation, tomes, or NPC teaching
    
- **Creatures, Monsters, and Ecosystems**
    
    Must integrate with ecology simulation, AI awareness, and physical interactions
    
- **Equipment & Craftables**
    
    Must balance with survival pacing, tool degradation, and crafting realism
    
- **Biomes & Worldgen Presets**
    
    Must remain within high-fantasy, quasi-realistic parameters (e.g., no vaporwave desert planets unless lore-justified)
    

### 🧑‍⚖️ **Future Ideas (Optional Enhancements)**

- **Weighted Voting by Mod Install Base + Feedback**
    
    Helps elevate useful mods that fit within aesthetic & gameplay norms
    
- **Player “Lore Tastes” Profiles**
    
    Auto-suggest mods based on player preferences (e.g., grounded realism, surreal myth, dark fantasy)
    
- **Self-Hosted "Mini-Councils"**
    
    Player groups or server hosts can adopt the tagging framework to curate their own trusted lists
    

---

In short, the Lore Council is *not a gatekeeper of stories* — it’s a **guidepost for creators and players alike**, helping *Infinite Worlds* grow without sacrificing tone, systemic integrity, or immersion.
