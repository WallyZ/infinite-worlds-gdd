# 3.10 World Physics & Planar Systems

In *Infinite Worlds*, not all regions, planes, or realities operate under the same physical, magical, or metaphysical rules. This system governs how different **rulesets** apply to **worlds**, **planes**, or **dimensions**, influencing everything from gravity and light to time flow, magic behavior, and player perception.

These rulesets allow for **planar travel**, **pocket realms**, and **alien physics**, introducing deep player discovery, adaptation, and survival gameplay. The system is modular and can affect local or global environments.

---

### 🧱 Core Principles

- **Localized Reality Frameworks**: Each world or region operates under its own rule definitions. These may be permanent, cyclical, or destabilized.
- **Player Awareness**: Rule shifts are learnable through observation, experimentation, or studying ancient/magical texts.
- **Systemic Integration**: The rulesets affect combat, crafting, physics, logic, perception, and metaphysics (e.g., soul behavior, memory persistence).

---

### ⚙️ Rule Types & Variables

Each layer may alter one or more of the following:

| Category | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| **Gravity & Motion** | Zero-g, reverse gravity, variable directional gravity, time-delayed motion |
| **Time Mechanics** | Time dilation, time loops, non-linear time, frozen time pockets |
| **Magic Behavior** | Wild magic zones, null zones, domain amplification, thought-powered effects |
| **Elemental Rules** | Dominance of specific elements (e.g., fire realm), elemental inversion |
| **Consciousness Effects** | Collective dreaming, reality tied to belief, altered perception filters |
| **Death & Rebirth** | Instant reincarnation, soul binding, permanent death, undeath tethering |
| **Information Decay** | Language shifts, memory instability, anti-knowledge zones |
| **Causality & Logic** | Rule of metaphor, story-logic dominance, inverse logic realms |

---

### 🌍 Example Planar Archetypes (Inspired by D&D)

Below are example types of planes/realms inspired by D&D’s cosmology and adapted to *Infinite Worlds*:

### 🜂 Elemental Dominions

- **The Ember Core**: Intense fire plane; water evaporates instantly, fire spells become stronger, organic matter decays faster.
- **Skyhold Drift**: Air realm with low gravity and floating landmasses; flight magic is stable, ranged weapons have unpredictable arcs.

### ⏳ Temporal Realms

- **The Timeless Hall**: Time is paused for external actors; players can move freely, but cause-effect chains only trigger after departure.
- **Mirrorstream**: Asynchronous time — actions taken echo backward or forward unpredictably.

### 🧠 Dream & Thought Planes

- **Oneiric Weave**: The dream realm where environments and outcomes are influenced by subconscious intent or psionic resonance.
- **Truthless Vault**: A mind-altering space where known facts become untrue unless continually re-asserted.

### 🌀 Wild Magic Zones

- **The Shatterfield**: Lawless magic plane — spells have 50% chance to mutate or fail unless stabilized with relics or rituals.
- **The Echo Veil**: Magic only works if tied to music or spoken poetry; silence causes nullification.

### ☠ Death & Memory Layers

- **The Bonewalk**: Souls walk visible trails; death leaves ghosts who remember everything, and can teach players.
- **Cenotaph Depths**: Memory deteriorates over time unless preserved; even the world forgets itself (dissolving terrain/NPCs).

---

### 🧪 Interaction & Adaptation Mechanics

- **Environmental Diagnostics**: Players can learn about altered rules via spells, alchemy, tools, or observation of anomalies.
- **Adaptation Systems**: Gear, spells, or behaviors may need modification (e.g., building tools that work under reverse gravity).
- **Rule Bending/Breaking**: Advanced players or rare psionic disciplines may allow temporary redefinition of local rules.

---

### 🧰 Tools for the Player (and DM-like Systems)

| Tool | Function |
| --- | --- |
| **Reality Lens** | Detects dominant physical/magical laws in the current zone |
| **Codex of Rulesets** | Player-built compendium of discovered laws, updated through gameplay or lore |
| **Psionic Stabilizers** | Anchor fragments of known reality in shifting zones |
| **Planar Glyphs** | Allow crafting of temporary safe-zones or reality-aligned bubbles |

---

### 🎲 Tabletop RPG Inspiration

This system draws inspiration from:

- **Planescape (D&D)**: Planes tied to belief, law/chaos balance, and cosmic alignment.
- **Mage: The Ascension**: Reality is subjective and enforced by consensus belief — rules are bendable by "Awakened" minds.
- **The Strange**: Each recursion/world has its own ruleset, affecting available skills, equipment, and logic.
- **Numenera**: Ancient tech and altered physics zones present exploratory challenges.

Just as tabletop GMs often warn players “this world doesn’t work like yours,” *Infinite Worlds* uses this to enrich its simulation, encouraging player curiosity, experimentation, and awe.
