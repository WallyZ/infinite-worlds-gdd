# 7.2.3 Campfire & Downtime Behavior

When not in immediate danger, companions express themselves through **organic, ambient behaviors** that enhance worldbuilding and deepen relationships.

**Key Features:**

- **Idle & Intentional Actions:**
    - Characters cook, tend to wounds, polish weapons, maintain armor, pace anxiously, sketch landscapes, or write in journals.
    - Patrols and watch rotations reflect character diligence or paranoia.
    - Activities vary depending on physical and emotional state (e.g., injured characters rest more, depressed ones isolate themselves).
- **Initiated Conversations:**
    - Companions may spontaneously start **context-sensitive dialogues**:
        - Share past traumas or personal memories.
        - Confide in the player, ask for advice, or reveal secrets.
        - Start arguments or engage in debates with other companions.
        - Tell stories, sing songs, or play games to lift morale.
- **Relationship Evolution:**
    - Quiet moments at camp influence the broader **narrative arc** of relationships:
        - New friendships or rivalries may form.
        - Hidden traits or backstories may emerge based on trust and time spent together.
