# 5.1.1 Physical Combat

### **Overview:**

Physical combat in *Infinite Worlds* is built around real-time VR motion and physics. Players must physically swing, parry, and dodge with their own body movements. The game uses hitboxes, momentum, and material-based interactions to simulate realistic fighting.

**Key Systems:**

- **Weapon Types & Handling:**
    - Swords, axes, spears, hammers, knives, whips, etc.
    - Different materials and weights affect swing speed and impact.
    - One-handed vs two-handed interactions.
- **Ranged Combat:**
    - Bows (manual drawing, arrow type selection), crossbows, slings, throwing knives.
    - Aim is hand- and eye-coordinated (not auto-aimed).
    - Arrows affected by gravity, wind, and material penetration.
- **Blocking & Parrying:**
    - Manual shield positioning or weapon blocks using timing and physics.
    - Parry windows vary by weapon type and enemy behavior.
- **Dodging & Footwork:**
    - VR movement integrated with physical crouching, sidestepping, or teleport-dodge if needed.
- **Unarmed Combat & Grappling:**
    - Punching, shoving, tackling, joint locks (with physics-based limb interaction).
    - Grappling requires stamina and can be countered by skill or leverage.
- **Armor & Encumbrance:**
    - Heavier armor reduces movement and affects stamina drain.
    - Can ricochet or absorb attacks based on material, angle, and weapon type.
- **Stamina & Injury Model:**
    - Swinging, dodging, blocking drain stamina.
    - Deep wounds, fractures, bleeding, and concussions handled via realistic health simulation.
    - Fatigue affects accuracy, reaction time, and ability to maintain combat effectiveness.
- **Weapon Maintenance:**
    - Blades dull, wooden shafts crack, strings snap.
    - Tools and knowledge required for upkeep.

## 🧰 Tips for Safe & Effective VR Combat Training

- **Ensure Adequate Space**: Clear a play area of at least 3x3 meters to safely perform dynamic movements.
- **Use Mixed Reality Features**: Apps with passthrough capabilities help maintain spatial awareness, reducing the risk of collisions.
- **Start Slow**: Begin with basic movements to acclimate to VR and prevent motion sickness.
- **Consider Full-Body Tracking**: For advanced training, invest in tracking equipment to capture leg and body movements accurately.

### ✅ **Requirements for Performing Physical Combat Forms in VR**

1. **Tracked Body Movement**
    - **Full-body tracking** is essential for kicks and spins.
    - This can be done using:
        - **VR trackers** (like HTC Vive trackers on feet, waist, etc.)
        - **External motion capture suits** (e.g., Rokoko, Xsens)
        - **Inside-out tracking with AI-based estimation** (e.g., Meta Quest with additional sensors or AI hand tracking)
2. **Spatial Awareness & Room Setup**
    - You need a **large, clear play area** (at least 3x3 meters) to avoid hitting furniture or walls.
    - Spinning in VR can be disorienting or dangerous if not carefully managed.
3. **Combat Form Design**
    - Moves need to be **modulated for safety** (e.g., limit height of kicks, controlled spins).
    - The combat system should offer **visual and audio cues** to guide movement safely.
4. **Software Support**
    - The VR app must:
        - Recognize and respond to full-body motion
        - Provide **real-time feedback** (e.g., comparing to a model form)
        - Possibly include **ghost forms** or trainers for mimicry

---

### ⚠️ **Challenges to Address**

- **Motion Sickness**
    - Spinning quickly in VR can cause severe nausea unless well-calibrated or tracked visually.
- **Lack of Haptic Feedback**
    - Striking "enemies" feels unsatisfying without physical resistance. Some setups use **resistance bands**, **force-feedback suits**, or **vibration feedback** to simulate contact.
- **Balance and Orientation**
    - Real-world balance is harder in VR, especially with **limited vision** (from a headset) and **latency** in tracking.
- **Safety**
    - High kicks and spins can cause users to lose balance or crash into objects. Tetherless systems (like Meta Quest) help, but clear physical space is vital.

---

### ✅ **Solutions/Approaches**

- **Mixed Reality (MR)**
    - Using passthrough (e.g., on Meta Quest 3), you can see your real room while interacting with virtual targets.
- **Augmented Combat Training Apps**
    - Some apps (like *Thrill of the Fight* or *Supernatural*) use controlled movement zones and rhythm rather than raw realism.
- **AI-Powered Coaching**
    - Apps could use AI to grade your form and adjust difficulty to match safe and realistic movements in your environment.
