# 1.11.1.8 Destiny & Destiny 2

Yes — *Destiny* and *Destiny 2* have many standout systems and design decisions that could inspire your VR sandbox RPG *Infinite Worlds*, **even though they’re FPS looter-shooters and not VR or fantasy games**. Their polish, modularity, worldbuilding, and gameplay feedback loops can offer **valuable lessons** across visuals, systems design, and player motivation.

Here's a breakdown of what *can* be adapted or reimagined for your VR fantasy sandbox RPG:

---

## ✅ WHAT TO ADAPT FROM DESTINY (1 & 2) FOR *INFINITE WORLDS*

---

### 🎮 **1. Weapon & Ability Feedback**

**Destiny's strength:** Every gun, melee, and ability *feels* incredible — weighty, punchy, clear.

### What to adapt for VR:

- **Tactile VR feedback:** Like controller haptics or hand recoil simulation for spellcasting or melee swings.
- **Distinct ability archetypes:** Class-based magic that feels instantly different per discipline (void bomb vs lightning stab).
- **Spellcasting FX layers:** Use layers of visuals (particles + sound + feedback + slowmo/camera shake) to make each spell cast feel *momentous*.

### 🧪 **2. Power Fantasy Loops with Limits**

**Destiny's loop:** You're powerful, but ability usage has cooldowns, charges, and class synergy.

### What to adapt:

- **Skill tree synergy:** Specializations that make one kind of spell or weapon truly shine.
- **Cooldowns that feel earned:** Use stamina, mana, focus, or breath for regulating power in immersive ways (think VR breath control for long-range aim).
- **Elemental damage feedback:** Fire feels like fire, ice slows, void pulls, etc. This overlaps well with your magic system.

### 🌐 **3. Diegetic UI & Immersive Feedback**

**Destiny's UI:** Sleek, minimal, very little HUD clutter.

### For VR immersion:

- Use **in-world indicators** (runes glowing brighter, weather changing subtly, hands pulsing with power).
- **No HUD magic wheel** — use gesture-based, physical cues like drawing a symbol in the air or gripping and "twisting" spells to activate variants.
- Inventory or quest info can be stored in a “magical journal” you physically open in VR.

### ⚔️ **4. Weapon & Gear Design Philosophy**

**Destiny's weapon/gun design is legendary** for:

- **Distinct visual/audio identity**
- **Random perks (rolls)** that create variety
- **Class or activity synergy** (a sword that recharges magic after kills, for example)

### For Infinite Worlds:

- Create **crafting or magical imbuing systems** that produce similar variance (e.g., a dagger made under a full moon gains shadow leech).
- Use **rituals or material memory** in VR to “build” items with emergent traits, not static stats.

### 🏹 **5. Combat Encounters and Flow**

**Destiny uses arena design and enemy variety** to:

- Encourage movement and ability use
- Mix small enemies (thralls) with elite enemies (knights, ogres, etc.)

### For your VR game:

- Don’t rely only on 1v1 duels — mix mob tactics with environment design.
- Let players kite, hide, climb, or manipulate terrain (e.g., setting traps or collapsing bridges).
- Vary enemy awareness via perception, noise, line of sight — not just aggro ranges.

### 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 **6. Cooperative Synergy & Roleplay Potential**

Destiny's fireteam classes (Titan, Hunter, Warlock) **encourage different playstyles and identities**.

### For Infinite Worlds:

- Encourage players to **build magical or practical identities**: the herbalist-alchemist, rune swordsmith, blood mage, etc.
- Let players **specialize based on real skills** (like navigation by stars, true herb recognition) — making your "skill-as-knowledge" system shine.

### 🧭 **7. World Design & Movement Flow**

**Destiny's environments:**

- Use skyboxes, color grading, and layout to make areas feel legendary.
- Each zone has **iconic landmarks** and shortcuts you discover with time.

### For your earth-sized fantasy world:

- Use **landmarking + worldbuilding** to make navigation natural, even without maps.
- Let players **build or uncover paths**, magical doorways, or climbing routes.
- Embed **narrative storytelling in the world itself**, not just quests.

## 🚫 What *Not* to Take from Destiny (or use with caution):

| Feature | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Pure loot grind | Doesn’t align with your more meaningful, skill-based progression. Avoid meaningless RNG. |
| Always-online service model | Not ideal unless you plan for persistent multiplayer; your game may focus more on immersion than live service. |
| Fast twitch shooting loops | Not suitable for VR unless you heavily adapt them for physical pacing and natural movement. |
| Fixed class systems | You want **fluid skill expression and real-world learning**, so avoid hard-locking roles too early. |
