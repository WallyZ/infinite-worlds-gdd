# 14.4 Milestones & Phases

### 🛠️ 1. **Build and Polish Your Game**

- **Development**: Use your preferred engine (like Unreal Engine 5) to build the game. Focus on performance, user comfort (especially for VR), and progression mechanics.
- **Testing**: Conduct internal QA and external playtests. Gather feedback and iterate.
- **Optimization**: Especially important for VR—optimize for frame rate, input latency, and comfort.

---

### 🌐 2. **Create a Web Presence**

- **Website**: Build a landing page with a trailer, screenshots, dev blog, and mailing list signup.
- **Social Media**: Start early. Share dev updates, behind-the-scenes content, and engage with your audience.
- **Community**: Consider Discord, Reddit, or Patreon to build a loyal following and gather feedback.

---

### 💰 3. **Monetization Strategy**

- **Pricing**: Choose between premium, free-to-play, or donation-based. For VR, premium or hybrid models often work well.
- **In-Game Store**: If applicable, integrate microtransactions carefully—especially in skill-based progression systems.
- **Platforms**: Decide where to sell—Itch.io, Steam, Epic Games Store, or your own site.

---

### 🛒 4. **Choose Distribution Platforms**

Here are some popular self-publishing options:

- [**Itch.io**](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/production/the-best-places-to-publish-your-indie-game): Great for indie and experimental games. You control pricing and revenue share.
- [**Steam**](https://www.game-developers.org/web-stories/how-to-self-publish-an-indie-game-from-start-to-finish/): Huge audience, but requires a $100 fee and passing Steamworks checks.
- [**Epic Games Store**](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/production/the-best-places-to-publish-your-indie-game): 88/12 revenue split, large user base, and open to Unreal and non-Unreal games.
- [**Humble Bundle**](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/production/the-best-places-to-publish-your-indie-game): Offers publishing support if accepted, with a 75/25 revenue split.

---

### 📣 5. **Marketing and Launch**

- **Press Kit**: Include game info, screenshots, logos, and contact details.
- **Trailers**: Create a gameplay trailer and teaser. These are crucial for store pages and social media.
- **Soft Launch**: Consider releasing a demo or early access version to gather feedback and build hype.
- **Launch Day**: Coordinate announcements, livestreams, and community events.

---

### 🧾 6. **Legal and Business Considerations**

- **Business Entity**: Register as an LLC or sole proprietorship for tax and liability purposes.
- **Copyright & Trademarks**: Protect your IP and assets.
- **Age Ratings**: Required for some platforms (e.g., ESRB, PEGI).
- **Licensing**: Ensure all third-party assets (music, plugins) are properly licensed.

---

### 🔄 7. **Post-Launch Support**

- **Updates**: Patch bugs, add content, and respond to feedback.
- **Community Engagement**: Keep your players involved with dev logs, polls, and events.
- **Analytics**: Use tools to track player behavior and retention.

---

- Pre-production: GDD, art tests, modular proof-of-concepts
- Alpha: core loops, full-world testbed
- Beta: full content, tuning, feedback
- Launch: optimization, marketing push, platform certification
- Post-launch: modding tools, new systems, world expansions
