# 12.1 Funding & Crowdsourcing

## 🎯 Core Positioning: "Educational Interactive Media"

Frame your game not just as entertainment but as:

- A **hands-on, immersive learning tool**
- A form of **applied art** combining interactivity, education, and storytelling
- A vehicle for **skill acquisition** through **play and discovery**

---

## 🧩 Specific Skills = Specific Value Propositions

| Real Skill in Game | Funding Framing Angle |
| --- | --- |
| **Plant identification** | Environmental literacy, botany education |
| **Crafting (realistic)** | STEM/STEAM learning, trades & vocational pathways |
| **Mapping, Navigation** | Geography, orienteering, spatial reasoning |
| **Language/Dialogue skills** | Communication, social interaction, or literacy support |
| **Survival systems** | Natural sciences, human biology, outdoor education |
| **Gesture-based spellcasting** | Kinesthetic learning, accessibility, cognitive engagement |

---

## 🧠 How to Frame It in Grant Applications

### ✅ 1. **For State Arts Agencies (like ASCA)**

- Describe your game as **interactive digital storytelling** with educational outcomes.
- Use terms like:
    - *"Immersive virtual reality experience blending real-world learning with fantasy adventure"*
    - *"Applied craft and exploration as both art and education"*
- Highlight:
    - Real-world skill immersion
    - Accessibility of learning through play
    - Cultural and artistic depth of systems

---

### ✅ 2. **For Educational or Public Impact Grants (e.g. NSF, NEA, NIH)**

- Emphasize **learning mechanics** as intentionally designed educational tools.
- Provide examples of **cognitive benefits**: spatial reasoning, pattern recognition, procedural learning.
- Propose **pilot partnerships** with schools, museums, or afterschool programs.
- Suggest assessments or outcomes tracking (even if just planned, not built yet).

---

### ✅ 3. **For Patreon / Crowdfunding**

- Offer exclusive “dev talks” on how the game teaches skills.
- Include tiers that let supporters vote on which **skills** you simulate next.
- Frame your dev journey as a public learning experience (like:
    - “We’re making fantasy real — come learn blacksmithing and herbalism with us, in VR.”)

---

### ✅ 4. **For Oculus Start / Meta Launch Pad / Accelerators**

- Highlight:
    - Skill-based immersion and retention: “This isn’t just a fantasy RPG — players *actually learn*.”
    - VR as a platform uniquely suited for muscle memory, procedural learning, and intuitive feedback.

---

## 🛠 Practical Tips for Applications

### 🔍 Keywords to Use in Grant Language

- **Immersive learning**, **procedural education**, **experiential knowledge**
- **Digital craft**, **interactive pedagogy**, **experiential simulation**
- **STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, Mathematics) education**, **interactive design**, **virtual apprenticeship**

### 📁 Artifacts to Include

- **Annotated gameplay videos** showing skill-based learning moments.
- **Testimonials or quotes** from early playtesters about what they learned.
- **Design documents** that reference real-world parallels (e.g., actual blacksmithing techniques, plant biology).
- **Curriculum-style outlines**: “By hour 5, a player will be able to recognize and safely prepare 3 local medicinal plants.”

---

## 🧠 Example Pitch Summary for a Grant

> “Infinite Worlds is an immersive VR sandbox RPG that transforms traditional gameplay into real-world learning. Players uncover practical skills such as navigation, survival techniques, crafting, and plant identification. Built with accessibility and deep engagement in mind, the game leverages spatial learning and procedural discovery to offer a novel approach to experiential education through interactive fantasy storytelling.”
>

- Transparent monetization goals (support sustainability, empower creativity)
- Avoidance of exploitative practices (no FOMO, pay-to-win, etc.)
- Balancing immersive world values with real-world needs
