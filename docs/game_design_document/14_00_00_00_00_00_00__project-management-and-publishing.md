# 14. Project Management & Publishing

### 🔹 Milestones / Timeline

> This timeline is flexible and may shift depending on workload, unforeseen challenges, or changes in scope.
>

🔹 **Prototype (Pre-Alpha) – Expanded**

**Estimated Duration:** *1–2 months (flexible)*

**Purpose:** Establish technical feasibility and validate the *core loop* of immersive interaction within a VR RPG setting. The prototype does not require complete visual polish but should focus on functional systems and player experience.

Add: Gantt chart or milestone table

---

### ✅ **Primary Goals**

1. **Core VR Interaction Mechanics**
    - Hand tracking and gesture-based control (via VR controllers or hand presence SDK)
    - Object pickup, throwing, inventory management via diegetic UI (e.g., backpack menu, holsters)
    - Contextual interactions (grabbing doors, levers, switches, books, etc.)
2. **Locomotion System(s)**
    - Teleportation, smooth locomotion, and/or hybrid movement options
    - Head-relative or controller-relative movement (based on comfort settings)
    - Optional features: climbing, crouching, ledge grabbing
3. **Combat Prototype (if applicable)**
    - Melee weapon interaction (e.g., sword physics, weapon collision)
    - Basic magic or ranged combat (e.g., hand gestures for spells, bow usage)
    - Enemy AI dummy (simple target or mannequin) for testing weapon impact
4. **UI / UX for VR**
    - Diegetic menu system (wristwatch UI, in-world HUD, or physical menu object)
    - Inventory interaction (quick access hotbar, item wheel, backpack you open with gestures)
    - Health/stats display that feels natural in VR (floating crystal, glowing veins, etc.)
5. **Interaction with World Elements**
    - Openable containers (chests, drawers)
    - Interactable props (books, maps, torches, potions)
    - Environmental physics (picking up, stacking, breaking objects)

---

### 🧪 **Key Prototype Deliverables**

- ✅ **Playable VR environment (small area or “test room”)**

    A confined but feature-rich area designed to test all interaction modes (e.g., cabin, ruins, or training hall)

- ✅ **Functioning player controller**

    Includes movement, hand interaction, and full body presence (if applicable)

- ✅ **Core loop test**

    A simple scenario that includes:

    - Equipping a weapon
    - Opening a chest
    - Picking up loot
    - Casting a basic spell
    - Fighting a dummy enemy
    - Accessing and managing inventory
- ✅ **Comfort options and calibration**
    - Adjustable movement settings (snap turning, movement smoothing)
    - Height calibration or room-scale testing

---

### 🎯 **Prototype Success Criteria**

- Player can complete a basic loop (explore → interact → equip → combat → loot)
- Interaction feels intuitive, responsive, and immersive in VR
- No motion sickness or disorientation during movement
- System runs at acceptable performance (~72+ FPS for Quest-class headsets)

---

### 💡 Optional Stretch Goals

- Haptic feedback (e.g., vibration when grabbing or striking)
- Voice command integration for spells or menus
- First-pass implementation of dialogue system (NPC with text or VO)
- Environmental storytelling (notes, murals, etc.)

---

This prototype serves as a **foundation for your vertical slice**, letting you decide what features are fun, what systems are feasible, and what needs refining. It’s also a great checkpoint to share short clips or gifs on social media to begin community-building.

- **Vertical Slice** *(2–3 months)*
    - One polished and representative segment of the game
    - Full audio/visual pass for selected environment
    - Showcases gameplay loop and tone
- **Alpha** *(2–3 months)*
    - All major systems implemented
    - Placeholder content mostly replaced
    - Core gameplay loop functional end-to-end
    - Begin gathering early feedback (friends, online dev communities)
- **Beta** *(1–2 months)*
    - Polish phase: bug fixing, visual and audio refinement
    - External playtesting (limited distribution or closed beta)
    - Performance optimizations
- **Launch** *(1 month)*
    - Final build preparation
    - Store page assets (trailers, screenshots, descriptions)
    - Marketing push (forums, social, press outreach)
    - Release on targeted platform(s)

> ✏️ Note: Post-launch updates and bug fixes will be tracked separately in a lightweight live support roadmap.
>

### 🔹 Team Roles

> This is a solo-developed project, so all roles are handled by the developer, with occasional support from external contractors as needed.
>
- **Game Design:** Player progression, mechanics, balance
- **Programming:** Core systems, tools, gameplay logic, bug fixing
- **Art & Animation:** Placeholder and final assets (2D/3D as applicable)
- **Audio:** Basic SFX and music (original or licensed/royalty-free)
- **Writing & Narrative:** Storyline, in-game text, dialogue
- **Marketing & Community:** Social media updates, devlogs, community interaction
- **QA:** Self-testing and community feedback collection

> 🧩 Potential outsourcing for:
>
> - Music composition
> - Specialized asset creation (e.g., detailed 3D models or UI icons)
> - Localization (post-launch)

---

### 🔹 Budget Estimates

> Operating under a limited budget, focused on minimizing expenses while maintaining quality where it matters most.
>
- **Game Engine & Tools:** $0–100
    - Open-source or free tools (Unity, Godot, Blender, GIMP, etc.)
- **Assets (Licenses & Store Packs):** $200–500
    - Purchased assets for prototyping or final use (Itch.io, Unity Asset Store, etc.)
- **Audio:** $50–150
    - Royalty-free music packs or budget for commissioned track(s)
- **Marketing:** $50–300
    - Trailer production (DIY or low-cost)
    - Social media ads or influencer micro-campaigns
- **Miscellaneous:** $50–100
    - Web hosting, domain registration, submission fees, etc.

> 🎯 Target Budget: $500–1,000 max (self-funded)
>

---

### 🔹 Risks & Contingencies

| **Risk** | **Mitigation** |
| --- | --- |
| Burnout or Time Constraints | Scope control, regular breaks, planning buffer between phases |
| Technical Scope Creep | Modular design, prioritize “must-have” vs “nice-to-have” features |
| Asset Limitations | Use placeholders early, focus polish on areas that impact gameplay most |
| Low Visibility at Launch | Build online presence early (devlog, Reddit, Itch, Twitter/X, etc.) |
| Bugs at Launch | Closed beta, dedicated bug-testing weeks before release |
| Lack of Feedback | Seek playtesters in indie forums, subreddits, Discord dev communities |

> ⚠️ Contingency Plan:
>
>
> If development stalls, pivot to releasing a small polished experience or demo (rather than full release) to gather interest or funding for continued development.
>
