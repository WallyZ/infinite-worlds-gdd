# 2.2.6 Milestones, Mastery & Nonlinear Progression

Progression is fully open-ended and arises from **player agency, skill expression, and world feedback**, not rigid quests or XP bars.

### 🧩 Player-Driven Objectives

- Players **create their own goals** through interaction with the world.
- The game may suggest opportunities, but **no objective is mandatory**.

Examples:

- Rebuild a ruined village and become its chieftain.
- Seek out and confront your long-lost sibling.
- Master a forgotten magic by traveling across civilizations.
- Join and reform a corrupt faction from within.

### 📜 Missions & Quests

- **Missions**: Found organically (e.g., “Wanted” boards, overheard conversations, personal notes).
- **Quests**: Dynamic story arcs that emerge through systems (not scripted fetch quests).
    - May form from NPC goals, faction struggles, or evolving world events.
    - Often require investigative thinking, moral decisions, or deep exploration.

### ⚒️ Progression Systems

| System | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Skill-Based Growth** | No leveling or XP. Skills improve through **repetition, experimentation, and training**. A sword swing gets better because you’ve practiced. |
| **Reputation & Social Memory** | NPCs remember your actions and statements. |
- Levels are replaced by skill mastery and social impact
- They may spread word (or lies) about you.
- Your reputation impacts access, trust, and relationships. |

    | **World Impact** | The world **remembers and evolves**:

- Cities rise or fall.
- Wars break out.
- Ecosystems change.
- Your influence shapes culture, religion, and power structures. |

- Major life events tracked (first shelter, lost companion)
- Flexible objectives based on curiosity or survival
- Mastery vs. novelty: mental plasticity vs. specialization
