# 4.1.2 Magic Schools & Disciplines

### **Magic Schools / Disciplines**

Magic in *Infinite Worlds* is not merely a game mechanic—it is a learned art, a discipline of will, ritual, and metaphysical understanding. Magic is categorized into **Schools**, also called **Disciplines**, each representing a fundamental paradigm through which reality may be altered. Mastery requires study, experimentation, attunement, and risk. Schools are not mutually exclusive; polymathic mages may blend Disciplines for unique effects.

Each Discipline governs a set of principles, tools, and symbolic languages (gestures, glyphs, spoken words) and reacts differently to the environment, practitioner mindset, and available magical infrastructure (ley lines, relics, dream states, etc.).

### 🌿 **5. Biomancy (Life, Growth, and Mutation)**

A form of naturalistic magic that influences biology—enhancing growth, accelerating healing, or altering physical forms. Used by herbalists, shamans, and druidic orders.

- **Sample Effects:** Regeneration, Barkskin, Spore Burst, Animal Communication
- **Cost:** Alters user biology over time; risk of cancerous growth, parasitism, or plant-animal hybridization.
- **Ritual Components:** Living matter, blood, seeds, lunar phases.

### 🌀 **6. Psionics (Mind, Thought, and Will)**

Though technically separate from traditional magic, Psionics is treated as a mental Discipline. It draws not from external energies but from internal reservoirs of will, emotion, and clarity.

- **Sample Effects:** Telepathy, Mind Push, Clairvoyance, Memory Reading
- **Requirements:** Mental discipline, emotional control, high cognitive load—risk of migraines, amnesia, madness.
- **Training Methods:** Meditation, lucid dreaming, psychic resonance objects.

### ⏳ **7. Chronomancy (Time and Causality)**

A rare and dangerous school dealing with time dilation, premonition, and causality loops. Not truly "time travel," but warping of perception and event probability.

- **Sample Effects:** Precognition, Time Slow, Moment Replay, Entropic Decay
- **Risks:** Paradox effects, temporal echoes, quantum entanglement with future/past versions of self.
- **Usually Restricted** to those trained by specialized sects or exposed to anomalous phenomena.

### 🌌 **8. Astral Magic (Dreams, Void, and Planar Travel)**

Magic that pierces the veil between worlds. Practitioners draw power from the Dreaming, the Void, or neighboring planes. Used in astral projection, summoning, and travel across realms.

- **Sample Effects:** Dreamwalk, Summon Entity, Create Rift, Void Gaze
- **Consequences:** Dream-corruption, possession by extraplanar entities, soul fragmentation.
- **Useful Items:** Sigils, planar maps, relics from other realms.

### 🔍 **9. Thaumaturgy (Symbolic Ritual & High Magic)**

Rooted in ancient theory, this school uses formal rituals, symbols, and components to invoke predictable magical effects. Unlike other Disciplines, Thaumaturgy is **slow**, deliberate, and stable.

- **Sample Effects:** Binding Contracts, Circle of Power, Transmutation Rites, Alchemical Constructs
- **Strengths:** Most versatile and precise; used to create enchanted items, wards, or complex effects.
- **Requirements:** Long casting times, rare components, precise language and timing.

### ✨ Hybrid & Forbidden Schools

Some practitioners transcend a single Discipline, combining elements into hybrid branches (e.g., **Pyrokinetic Telepathy**, **Necroillusion**). Others pursue **forbidden schools**—arts lost to time or sealed due to existential risk (e.g., **Blood Magic**, **Name Magic**, **Soulforging**).

### 🧭 Progression & Learning

- **Discovery:** Magic is learned through grimoires, mentors, trial and error, and ancient relics. Visual effects and spell outcomes are vague until mastered.
- **Skill-Based Casting:** Proficiency affects control, reliability, and energy cost. Spells may partially succeed, misfire, or morph into unintended forms.
- **Diegetic Interface:** Casting relies on gestures, spoken words, ritual tools, and learned body language—no menus or hotkeys. A novice mage may take a full minute to complete a ritual, while a master can cast it reflexively.
- **Environmental Influence:** Ley lines, weather, planetary alignments, and biome affect spell performance and availability.
