# 3. Simulation & World Systems

## 🧭 Core Philosophy

The world is a **massive, procedurally generated living sandbox**, designed for **deep immersion, exploration, and narrative emergence**. Each new game spawns a **completely unique world**, complete with its own **geography, cultures, conflicts, myths, and history**. No two playthroughs will ever be the same.

---

### 🌐 World Generation Overview

- **Procedural Megastructure:**
    - The world is generated similarly in scale and biome complexity to Earth.
    - Includes continents, islands, oceans, mountain ranges, deserts, forests, rivers, cave systems, tundras, jungles, and underground networks.
    - Biomes affect travel, resources, visibility, and regional culture.
- **Geological & Climatic Systems:**
    - Plate tectonics simulate natural land formations.
    - Climate is influenced by latitude, elevation, proximity to oceans, and magical anomalies (e.g., Everstorm Zones, Eternal Winters).
    - Seasonal cycles and weather patterns are dynamic and affect gameplay (visibility, crops, monster spawning, etc.).
- **Seeded Uniqueness:**
    - A world seed determines the configuration of landmasses, civilizations, events, and histories.
    - A saved seed can be shared, but every player begins with a different one by default.

---

### 🏰 Civilizations & Settlements

- **City-State Generation:**
    - Cities, towns, villages, nomadic camps, ruins, and ancient strongholds populate the world based on historical development logic (e.g., along rivers, near trade routes, in fertile valleys).
    - Each is tied to a local or regional **culture**, **language**, **religion**, **architectural style**, and **government system**.
- **Racial Distribution:**
    - Sentient species (humans, elves, dwarves, beastkin, etc.) are distributed naturally based on world history and terrain.
    - Race-specific tensions, alliances, and migrations occur due to events (e.g., ancient wars, climate catastrophe, divine intervention).
- **Political Landscape:**
    - Factions rise and fall dynamically during world gen: empires, republics, theocracies, nomadic tribes, pirate enclaves, underground resistance networks.
    - Factional relations (neutrality, trade, cold war, active war) shape the living world.
    - Players can affect this geopolitics through interaction, subterfuge, or conquest.

---

### 🛤️ Region Types

- **Urban Centers**:
    - Bustling hubs of politics, trade, and intrigue.
    - NPCs with complex schedules, economies, social hierarchies.
    - Quests here may involve guilds, heists, politics, or social dilemmas.
- **Frontier Settlements**:
    - Recently established towns on contested ground.
    - Danger from monsters, warbands, or natural disasters.
    - Quests include defense, establishment, diplomacy, and survival.
- **Wilderness Zones**:
    - Dense forests, deep caves, high peaks, vast deserts.
    - Rare ingredients, ancient ruins, dangerous creatures.
    - Exploration-heavy with emergent encounters and survival mechanics.
- **Sacred / Magical Sites**:
    - Tied to ancient powers or deities.
    - May include puzzles, guardians, or prophecies.
    - Important for character progression or major story events.

---

### 🏛️ Historical Simulation

- **Myth Generator**:
    - Each world has a generated history, myths, heroes, and disasters spanning thousands of years.
    - Includes ancient wars, fallen empires, religious schisms, divine calamities, and arcane breakthroughs.
    - Players may uncover lost knowledge or interact with long-dead factions through artifacts or ruins.
- **Cultural Memory**:
    - NPC attitudes are influenced by their people's history (e.g., distrust of elves due to a past betrayal).
    - This can affect dialogue, trade, alliances, and questlines.

---

### 🧭 Navigation & Exploration

- **Fog of War**:
    - The map is revealed through exploration or rumor.
    - Cartographers and explorers can sell or trade maps.
- **Dynamic Travel Encounters**:
    - Travel is not just fast-travel. Players can experience:
        - Ambushes or aid from locals or creatures.
        - Environmental events (storms, magical distortions).
        - Caravan routes, smugglers, or hidden paths.
- **Mounts & Vehicles**:
    - Horses, ships, flying creatures, or magical transport are unlockable for faster or specialized travel.
    - Each has region-based availability and upgrade paths.

---

### 🧩 Level Design Approach

- **Modular Encounter Zones**:
    - World content is made up of modular “puzzle pieces” such as enemy outposts, ancient ruins, magical springs, or story vignettes.
    - These are placed in contextually appropriate locations during world gen.
- **Procedural-Directional Hybrid**:
    - Broad world structure is procedural, but key story dungeons and main questline areas are handcrafted and anchored to logical points within the world.
    - A system ensures accessibility and relevance of narrative progression despite procedural variation.

---

### 🔧 World Interaction Systems

- **Environmental Manipulation**:
    - Players can influence terrain via magic, construction, or destruction.
    - Over time, this can change the accessibility or danger level of regions.
- **Reputation & Influence**:
    - Player choices ripple across regions.
    - Saving a town may lead to invitations, marriage offers, or trade discounts.
    - Destroying a fortress may shift borders or cause power vacuums.
- **Living World AI**:
    - NPCs move, trade, fight, marry, age, and die based on a life simulation loop.
    - The world does not wait for the player: wars may be lost, cities overthrown, or dynasties extinguished without intervention.

---

### ✨ Optional Additions for Development

- **World Codex / Lore Compendium**:
    - Auto-generates a readable lorebook of the generated world’s history, cultures, races, pantheons, and political events.
    - Can be unlocked through exploration, archaeology, or bard NPCs.
- **Alternate Planes / Realms**:
    - Dreamworlds, hellscapes, divine realms, and magical overlays can be linked to geography and story events.
    - Some regions might phase in/out of reality, or be visible only through magic.
