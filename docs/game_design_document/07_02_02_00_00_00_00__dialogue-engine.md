# 7.2.2 Dialogue Engine

The dialogue system supports **adaptive, reactive conversations** that reflect character complexity and the evolving state of the party.

**Key Features:**

- **Dynamic Personality Matrix:**
    - Dialogue tone and choices are shaped by traits like optimism, suspicion, sarcasm, compassion, and ambition.
    - Emotional state (e.g., stressed, grieving, euphoric) changes inflection, vocabulary, and decision-making in real time.
- **Relational Context:**
    - Companions speak differently depending on their **rapport with the player**:
        - A friendly rogue teases affectionately.
        - A resentful mage keeps things curt or combative.
        - A romantic interest might offer unique lines, voice tones, or branching quests.
- **Moral Alignment and Party Tension:**
    - Dialogue responses can clash with others, triggering interjections, side arguments, or loyalty conflicts.
    - Party dynamics shift as companions **react to your choices** in past quests or in-the-moment dialogue.
- **Memory-Based Reactions:**
    - Characters remember key events and reference them later, e.g.:
        - “You left me behind in the ruins—why would I trust you now?”
        - “That night by the fire, you said you believed in me. I won’t forget it.”
