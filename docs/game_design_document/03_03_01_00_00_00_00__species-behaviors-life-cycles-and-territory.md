# 3.3.1 Species Behaviors, Life Cycles & Territory

**Core Philosophy**

The ecosystem reacts to player interactions, fostering **complex food chains, biome-specific behaviors, and environmental storytelling**.

**Biome-Specific Ecology**

- **Jungle:** Dense canopy ecosystems with vine-climbing predators and intelligent pack hunters.
- **Tundra:** Creatures adapted to harsh winters, incorporating **hibernation mechanics and seasonal activity cycles**.
- **Ocean:** A **living underwater world** with reefs, deep-sea leviathans, and adaptable migrations.
- **Cave Networks:** Unique **bioluminescent flora, subterranean predators, and mineral-dependent lifeforms**.

**Dynamic Interactions**

- **Adaptive Prey-Predator Relationships:** AI-driven creatures **hunt, migrate, and evolve** based on scarcity and environmental shifts.
- **Weather & Environmental Effects:** Flooding, droughts, wildfires, and magical anomalies **reshape terrain and local wildlife behaviors**.
- **Player Influence on Ecosystems:** **Overhunting, deforestation, or magical alterations** may permanently shift the balance of regions, leading to **extinction events or invasive species outbreaks**.

Dig deeper into predator-prey loops, migration patterns, and how gameplay mechanics (e.g., hunting, farming, magic pollution) alter the balance. Useful if you integrate survival or ecosystem restoration elements.
