# 1.11.1.5 Kingdom Come: Deliverance

### 📅 Overview

- **Developer:** Warhorse Studios (Czech Republic)
- **Publisher:** Deep Silver
- **Engine:** CryEngine
- **Genre:** Open-world, first-person, historical RPG
- **Setting:** Bohemia (Holy Roman Empire), 1403

---

## 💡 TIMELESS STRENGTHS AND CORE DESIGN IDEALS

### 1. **Historical Realism as Core Design**

- No dragons, elves, or magic. Just real medieval politics, poverty, warfare, and religion.
- The game simulates:
    - Armor layering
    - Weapon physics
    - Noble vs peasant class dynamics
    - Church influence
    - Language (some Old Czech, Latin, German)
- Even **architecture and dialects** are based on historical records.

**🧠 Lesson for Infinite Worlds:**

- *Fantasy doesn't need to be whimsical.* You can build mythic depth from real-world systems, forgotten knowledge, and historical analogues.
- Aim for **anthropological fantasy**—root your cultures, religions, magic, and conflicts in plausible material and societal evolution.

---

### 2. **First-Person Embodiment**

- Full body awareness: you see your feet, weapons, clothing, hands.
- Food must be cooked or can make you sick. Sleep is mandatory. You can stink if you don’t bathe.
- Armor must be worn in layers; damage to gear affects effectiveness.
- You need to read books to learn, but only after your character has learned to read.

**🧠 Lesson:**

- Embodiment and **physicality** are powerful. In VR, this goes even further.
- Design your mechanics to *reward slow, physical interaction*—grabbing ingredients, applying salves, actually reading pages, maintaining tools.

---

### 3. **Skills-as-Knowledge and Realism-First Progression**

- You must **learn to read** before books make sense.
- Alchemy uses **step-by-step physical interactions** (e.g., grinding herbs, boiling for exact durations).
- Persuasion is based on **character reputation, voice tone, social status**, and **appearance** (e.g., dirty clothes = disrespect).
- Lockpicking, pickpocketing, and combat are all difficult but satisfying once mastered.

**🧠 Lesson:**

- Echo this in *Infinite Worlds*:
    - Skills unlock perception (identifying herbs, stars, dangers).
    - Use **layered realism**: you don’t just “know alchemy”—you must observe, test, read, and practice.
    - *Let mastery feel real*, not abstracted.

---

### 4. **Combat as a Martial Simulation**

- Melee combat uses:
    - Directional attacks and blocks
    - Feints and ripostes
    - Stamina and momentum systems
- Armor works realistically; arrows bounce off plate.
- Timing, footwork, and reading your opponent are crucial.

**🧠 Lesson:**

- Build a **VR martial combat system** inspired by HEMA (historical European martial arts).
- Weight, positioning, and stamina matter more than “DPS.”

---

### 5. **Consequences, Reputation, and Social Simulation**

- Reputation is local, tied to villages, factions, and class.
- Being caught stealing lowers trust. Crimes must be paid in jail time, coin, or honor loss.
- Dialogue and access to quests change based on:
    - Your clothes (peasant vs noble garb)
    - Dirtiness and injuries
    - Prior actions and faction alignment

**🧠 Lesson:**

- Build **multi-layered reputation systems**:
    - Local and global reputation
    - Faction alignment
    - Dynamic NPC reactions
- These should persist, influence quests, pricing, dialogue, and story.

---

### 6. **Immersive Quest & Dialogue Design**

- No magical waypoints—NPCs give verbal directions.
- Many quests can be completed multiple ways:
    - Sneaking
    - Bribing
    - Persuasion
    - Reading ancient documents
- Realistic pacing—quests unfold over days, with downtime.

**🧠 Lesson:**

- Design for **naturalistic quests**:
    - Give verbal or visual cues, not UI markers.
    - Provide multiple, systemic approaches to any problem.
    - Use **language, disguise, timing, disguise, translation**, or **skill** as solutions.

---

### 7. **Alchemy & Crafting as Hands-On Processes**

- Alchemy requires:
    - Grinding herbs
    - Using correct vessels
    - Boiling, distilling, filtering—**all in real time**
- Failure leads to waste; success yields valuable potions with real-world properties (valerian, nettle, belladonna).

**🧠 Lesson:**

- You can **teach real-world knowledge** through fantasy.
- Crafting should be interactive and skill-based, not menu-driven—especially in VR.
- Let feedback (color, smell, effect) teach correct use.

---

### 8. **Slow Burn Progression & Long-Term Consequences**

- Early game is harsh—Henry is weak, untrained, and poor.
- Over time, players slowly **transform through effort**.
- You carry scars, guilt, stolen gear, memories of war, and changed relationships.

**🧠 Lesson:**

- Don’t fear slow pacing.
- Allow *players to struggle*—so that growth and victory feel earned.
- Let the world remember them through scars, legacies, monuments, changed NPC relationships.

## 📉 SHORTCOMINGS (and how to improve them in your game)

| Weakness | Why It Matters | Fix for Infinite Worlds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Clunky UI** | Inventory & skill management were dense, not intuitive | Use **VR inventory metaphors** (bags, belts, pouches), physical interfaces, hand-based menus |
| **Stiff Animation & Combat AI** | Enemy reactions sometimes lag or glitch | Design with **VR-native AI patterns**: distance sensing, body posture reactions, breathing/fatigue |
| **Limited Magic or Mysticism** | It was a historical game, so magic was excluded | Your game can **expand realism to include mythic logic**—rituals, alchemy, stargazing, symbols |
| **Rigid Story Sequence** | Mostly linear narrative with limited branching | Lean into **emergent story arcs** shaped by player action, timing, geography, faction alignment |

## 🧩 APPLICABLE MODULES FOR *INFINITE WORLDS*

| System | How to Adapt |
| --- | --- |
| **Hands-on Alchemy** | Fully VR-compatible physical crafting: mortar/pestle, boiling, sniffing, labeling, testing |
| **First-Person Physical Survival** | Need for warmth, water, rest, hygiene, injury—each with **real feedback loops** |
| **Armor & Weapon Realism** | Weight, layers, momentum, rust, maintenance—affected by climate, magic, and smithing skill |
| **Class-Based Reputation** | NPCs judge player by appearance, accent, symbols worn, or magic used |
| **Language & Reading** | Books, runes, and ancient scripts are illegible until learned—can be a **core mechanic** for discovery |
| **Crafting = Real Skill** | Not menus, but *interaction*: pull sinew, braid rope, bind runes, carve talismans |
| **Law, Crime & Punishment** | Local authorities react; crime changes perception; court systems or vigilante justice possible |

## 🔭 WHY *KCD* HAS LASTED SO LONG

| Factor | Explanation |
| --- | --- |
| **Historical Fidelity** | Few other games go this deep into non-fantasy medieval life |
| **Systems-Based Design** | Interlocking systems of survival, speech, combat, alchemy, crime |
| **Realistic Challenge** | You *feel* weak, dirty, scared—immersion through difficulty |
| **Narrative Immersion** | High-quality writing, voice acting, and branching dialogue with real weight |
| **Modding & Community** | Fans have added armors, difficulty tweaks, free-roam modes |
| **Self-Education Value** | You *learn medieval literacy, medicine, ethics* while playing |

## 🧠 SUMMARY TAKEAWAYS

### ✅ Embrace:

- Full-body, skill-based interaction (especially in VR)
- Realistic social systems (clothes, class, language affect outcome)
- Gradual mastery through effort and discovery
- Crafting that mirrors real-world understanding
- Systems simulation that *feels* mythic without needing fantasy tropes

### 🛠️ Improve:

- Nonlinear story paths and branching consequences
- Deeper AI simulation: relationships, memory, rivalries
- VR-specific adaptations for inventory, alchemy, books, combat
- Expand realism to include **symbolic or magical realism** rather than strict historicity
