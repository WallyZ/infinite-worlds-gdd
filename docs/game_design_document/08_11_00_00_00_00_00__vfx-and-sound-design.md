# 8.11 VFX & Sound Design

### 🎵 **Music Style**

- The musical score will be **ambient and reactive**, dynamically adjusting to the **emotional tone** and **player actions** within the environment.
- Music will shift between subtle, moody layers in exploration or downtime, to more intense or discordant motifs in combat or high-stakes scenarios.
- Each biome or region may have a unique musical theme, incorporating **diegetic elements** (e.g., native instruments, environmental sounds).
- Compositions will be designed for **seamless transitions**, enabling mood shifts without interrupting immersion.

### 🔉 **SFX Categories**

Sound effects will be broken down into the following categories:

- **Environmental Audio:**
    - Wind, rain, rustling leaves, water, echoes in caves, ambient wildlife.
    - Realistic foley elements recorded and modified for fantasy realism.
- **Player Interactions:**
    - Movement (footsteps by terrain type, gear shifting), grabbing, crafting sounds, spellcasting.
    - Contextual sounds for interactions like pulling levers, flipping through tomes, or carving.
- **Creatures & NPCs:**
    - Unique sounds per species/character to enhance identification and immersion.
    - Layered vocalizations for monsters and magical entities.
- **Combat FX:**
    - Weapon clashes, blocking, impact reactions, and magical sound layers.
    - Positional 3D audio cues for VR spatial awareness.
- **UI/UX Feedback:**
    - Subtle cues for menu navigation, selections, alerts, and system responses.
    - Audio used to reinforce feedback in accessibility modes.

### 🗣️ **Voice Acting Needs**

- All character dialogue will be generated via **AI voice synthesis**, with these guidelines:
    - Each major character will have a **distinct voice profile**, emotion range, and speaking style.
    - No two characters will sound alike — dialect, pacing, and tone will be adjusted for individuality.
    - Emotional and situational inflection will be used for realism (e.g., stress, fatigue, urgency).
    - Dynamic VO generation may allow procedural conversations or journals to be spoken aloud in-world.

### 🎚️ **Audio Middleware**

- **Primary Tool:** Wwise, FMOD or CRI ADX LE will be used for base-level sound design, cleaning, layering, and audio management.
- **In-Engine Audio System:**
    - Unreal Engine 5.6’s **MetaSounds** and **Quartz Subsystem** will be utilized for advanced in-game audio control and adaptive composition.
- **Spatialization:**
    - Native **3D spatial audio** support (e.g., HRTF) to match VR positional awareness.
    - Binaural audio cues will be carefully calibrated to help with navigation, stealth, and atmosphere.
