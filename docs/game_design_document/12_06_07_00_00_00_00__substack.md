# 12.6.7 Substack

### ✅ **Why Substack?**

- **Email-first platform**: You own your audience — unlike social media, you’re not subject to algorithm changes.
- **Built-in discoverability**: Readers can find your publication through categories, recommendations, and cross-promotions.
- **Long-form friendly**: Perfect for deep dives into game design, lore, worldbuilding, or philosophy behind your systems.
- **Monetization ready**: You can offer paid tiers, early access, dev diaries, or behind-the-scenes content.

---

### 🔧 **Tactical Ways to Use Substack for Game Growth**

### 1. **Developer Diary (Devlog) Series**

- Share **biweekly updates** on design progress, major systems (e.g., crafting, magic, survival), and roadblocks.
- Include images, videos, prototype GIFs, and **behind-the-scenes reasoning** for your game design choices.
- Great for showcasing transparency and building trust.

> 🧠 Tie-ins: Discuss how your game teaches real-world skills. Readers interested in philosophy, simulation, or TTRPGs will stay.
>

---

### 2. **Lore & Worldbuilding Drops**

- Post serialized **short stories**, **in-world documents**, or **character spotlights**.
- Use cliffhangers and recurring characters to **hook narrative-focused readers**.
- Offer visual lore maps, images, or early lore drafts to paid subscribers.

> 🔁 Cross-promote this content with your game’s wiki, Discord, or site.
>

---

### 3. **Design Deep Dives & Game Philosophy**

- Share essays on topics like:
    - "What Makes Realism Fun?"
    - "Permadeath and Player Psychology"
    - "Simulated Societies: NPCs with Souls"
- These attract **fellow game devs, writers, and thinkers** who will amplify your reach.

> 📚 Bonus: Tie in relevant books, TTRPG theory, or neuroscience to give it weight.
>

---

### 4. **Community Engagement & Polling**

- Use subscriber polls or comments to ask:
    - “What kind of foraging system would you prefer?”
    - “Which culture in Infinite Worlds do you want to explore next?”
- Let readers vote on features, cosmetic styles, or even names for in-game items/NPCs.

> 💬 Creates buy-in and boosts organic sharing.
>

---

### 5. **Early Access & Patreon Tie-ins**

- Offer:
    - **Early dev builds**
    - **Alpha invites**
    - **Exclusive content drops**
- Integrate Substack with your **Patreon**, giving overlapping or unique value (like exclusive lore PDFs or dev Q&As).

> 📈 Pro Tip: Treat paid Substack like a “collector’s club” — rare art, secrets, early mechanics.
>

---

### 6. **Cross-Promotion & Guest Features**

- Do guest posts with other indie devs or creators who share your values (e.g., simulation, RPG depth, worldbuilding).
- Invite known thinkers (e.g., TTRPG GMs, sci-fi/fantasy authors, VR designers) to write guest essays or Q&As.

> 🔄 This expands your reach and increases your authority.
>

### 🚀 Tools to Grow It Faster

- **Use lead magnets**: Offer a free downloadable PDF like "Infinite Worlds: World Primer" or a visual map of your magic system to new subscribers.
- **Substack Recommendations**: Recommend similar creators so they recommend you.
- **Share Substack links across devlogs, Kickstarter, Discord, and forums**.
